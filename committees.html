In the real world, the governments are required to deal with certain situations that cannot be handled in a calm methodical setting of a committee. Thus, it asks for the members to be quick on the uptake so that they can prioritise and wheedle their way until their interests are the ones first served.

The Human Rights Council is an inter-governmental body within the United Nations system which is responsible for strengthening the promotion and protection of human rights around the globe and for addressing situations of human rights violations and make recommendations on them. It has the ability to discuss all thematic human rights issues and situations that require its attention throughout the year. It meets at the UN Office at Geneva.

The Council is made up of 47 United Nations Member States which are elected by the UN General Assembly.

The Human Rights Council replaced the former United Nations Commission on Human Rights. United Nations Committee on the Peaceful Uses of Outer Space




Agenda

Discussing the Framework for delimitation of Outer Space In 2045

COPUOS is the UN Committee on the peaceful uses of outer space. It works under the guidance of UNOOSA. Set up by the General Assembly in 1959 through resolution 1348 to govern the exploration and use of space, it is for the benefit of all humanity for peace, security, and development. It has been instrumental in the creation of five treaties and five principles of outer space. But the dire need for diplomacy shines as the committee is in the year 2045. Science has made man capable of colonising Mars, and humanity has reached a new high of interplanetary travel. Owing to rapid technology and changing dynamics of international politics, will the member states be able to curb the growing hostilities regarding space divisions?

Will they be able to ensure the growing expansion and distribution of celestial bodies does not fuel the intensification among the rising hegemonic powers? The answer lies in the journey of putting forward the member states' interests through debates and negotiations. Owing to the fact that it has become critical and urgent to curb the international hostilities which have taken a toll on the planetary forum.

United Nations Women




Agenda

Formulating a Roadmap for Gender Equality and Promoting Women's Empowerment

Gender equality is not only a basic human right, but its achievement has enormous socio-economic ramifications. Empowering women fuels thriving economies, spurring productivity and growth. Yet gender inequalities remain deeply entrenched in every society. Women lack access to decent work and face occupational segregation and gender wage gaps. They are too often denied access to basic education and health care. Women in all parts of the world suffer violence and discrimination.

They are under-represented in political and economic decision-making processes. UN Women is the United Nations entity dedicated to gender equality and the empowerment of women. A global champion for women and girls, UN Women was established to accelerate progress on meeting their needs worldwide. UNW supports UN Member States as they set global standards for achieving gender equality, and works with governments and civil society to design laws, policies, programmes and services needed to ensure that the standards are effectively implemented and truly benefit women and girls worldwide. Through this platform, we at JMUNC, desire to provide with young delegates to come, bring out discussions and debates towards empowerment of women through Gender Sensitisation. Gender sensitisation promotes equality for men and women by allowing men and women to view what is stereotypical of and reasonable for their gender.

A common known process, that the world needs to understand, and implementing that internationally, is what essence of the meet. Combining, global good for women, with diplomacy and cooperation at the highest level, is our aim.
